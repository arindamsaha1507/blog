In the [[Grandness of Sanskrit Grammar Explained Briefly|previous article]], we saw that a list of all meaningful words is **pretty** long. So long that it took divine entities thousands of cosmic years to perhaps completely list it out. Moreover, I claimed in BLOCK LETTERS, that

> It takes a FINITE set of starting building blocks (about 2000 verb roots, 1000 suffixes, and a few hundred ready-made words) and a FINITE set of formulae/rules (less than 5000) to create an INFINITE number of possible words.

But to this you may ask this very logical question,

> So you claim that there are infinitely many meaningful words in Sanskrit? Like *really* infinite? Not millions, billions, or quadrillions, but even more? But I can definitely access Sanskrit dictionaries and even the largest of them have less than a million words.
